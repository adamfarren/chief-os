## Their Strengths

**Be honest — these are real advantages:**

1. **Segment breadth.** 12 segments with deep, purpose-built workflows. Businesses that need one platform across multiple segments often choose CoreLedger because they can cover everything. We can't yet.

2. **Enterprise maturity.** Multi-entity admin, role-based permissions, cross-entity analytics — they've had this for 3 years. Large groups trust them for this. Our enterprise tier ships in June.

3. **Customer base and references.** ~400 customers means they have references in almost every segment and geography. In competitive deals, they can always produce a relevant reference.

4. **Implementation team.** 40+ implementation specialists. They white-glove the deployment. We rely on a smaller team + self-service, which works for SMB but scares enterprise buyers.

## Their Weaknesses

1. **Monolithic architecture.** On-prem components remain for ~30% of their customer base. Cloud migration has been slow. This creates deployment complexity, slower releases, and higher support costs. [Source: Glassdoor engineering reviews mention "legacy codebase" frequently, SIMULATED]

2. **Bolt-on AI.** Their AI partnership (announced Q3 2025) is a wrapper around a third-party NLP service. It doesn't have access to the full transaction record in real-time — it processes documents after the fact. Controllers report it's "useful but not transformative." [Source: G2 reviews, SIMULATED]

3. **No developer platform.** Customers who want custom workflows must submit feature requests and wait. Average feature request turnaround: 6-12 months. Our Agent SDK lets them build it themselves in weeks.

4. **Slow release cadence.** Quarterly releases (4/year) vs. our continuous deployment. Customer feedback loop is slow. [Source: CoreLedger changelog shows 4 releases in 2025, SIMULATED]

## Our Differentiation

Tied to Meridian Ledger capabilities from company.yaml:

- **AI-native from day one.** Our AI isn't bolted on — it's the architecture. This means real-time categorization, native agent capabilities, and AI that has full context of the transaction record.
- **Agent SDK — they can't match this.** An open developer platform for financial AI agents. Their closed architecture makes this structurally impossible without a rewrite.
- **Faster time to value.** 4-6 weeks vs. 12-16 weeks. Cloud-native means no on-prem headaches.
- **130% NDR.** Our customers expand. Their estimated NDR is ~110% — good but not platform-level retention.

## Key Talking Points

1. **Platform vs. product.** "CoreLedger is a product. We're a platform. Their customers wait for features. Our customers build them." Back this up with the Agent SDK and a specific example (the variance analysis agent built in 2 weeks).
2. **AI depth, not AI decoration.** "Their AI is a partnership announcement. Ours is the architecture." Ask the prospect to compare a real-time AI categorization session side by side.
3. **Speed of innovation.** "We've shipped more AI features in 6 months than they have in 2 years. That pace doesn't slow down — it accelerates with the developer ecosystem."
