# Venture Debt Comparison: Summit Lending vs. Westbridge Capital

**2026-04-08 | Prepared for: Jordan Rivera**

## Executive Summary

**Recommend Westbridge Capital.** The larger facility ($7M + $2M revolver), longer draw period (12 months vs. 6), and lower revenue covenant ($8M vs. $10M) give Meridian Ledger more flexibility during the Series C process. The higher all-in cost (~$450K more over the term) is worth paying for the optionality. Negotiate the warrant coverage down from 0.40% to 0.30% and the prepayment penalty to match Summit's structure.

## Risk Assessment

### Summit Lending Risks
- **Revenue covenant at $10M ARR is tight.** Current ARR is $12.5M but the $10M floor leaves only 20% margin. A bad quarter could trigger a breach.
- **6-month draw period is short.** If the Series C takes longer than expected, you can't draw additional capital after month 6.
- **Fixed rate is a double-edged sword.** Protects against rate increases but you can't benefit from rate decreases.

### Westbridge Capital Risks
- **Variable rate exposure.** If Prime increases 100bps, annual interest cost increases by $70K on the full $7M.
- **Higher prepayment penalty.** 3% in year 1 = $210K on $7M. If the Series C closes quickly and you want to clean up the cap table, this is expensive.
- **Gross margin covenant (70%).** This is unusual for venture debt. If you invest heavily in infrastructure or support during enterprise expansion, margins could compress temporarily.
- **Higher warrant coverage.** 0.40% is on the high end of market. At a $200M exit, this is $800K in dilution vs. $500K for Summit.

## Negotiation Playbook

### Priority 1: Reduce Westbridge warrant coverage
**Ask:** 0.25% (matching Summit)
**Target:** 0.30%
**Minimum acceptable:** 0.35%
**Reasoning:** "We have a competing term sheet at 0.25%. We like the Westbridge structure but need the warrant coverage to be competitive."
**Trade:** Accept the 2.0% closing fee without negotiation. That's $140K now vs. $300K+ in warrant value over time.

### Priority 2: Restructure prepayment penalty
**Ask:** Match Summit's structure (2%/1%/0%)
**Target:** 2.5%/1.5%/0.5%
**Minimum acceptable:** 3%/1.5%/0% (accelerated decline)
**Reasoning:** "We're actively raising our Series C. There's a realistic scenario where we prepay within 12 months. The current structure penalizes a fast close."
**Trade:** Offer to commit to the full $7M draw (no partial draw option) in exchange for better prepayment terms.

### Priority 3: Remove gross margin covenant
**Ask:** Remove entirely, keep only the ARR floor
**Target:** Reduce to 65% floor
**Minimum acceptable:** 65% floor with 1-quarter cure period
**Reasoning:** "We're investing in enterprise infrastructure that may temporarily compress margins. The ARR covenant already protects you — adding a margin covenant restricts our ability to invest in growth."

### Walk-Away Position
Accept Summit if Westbridge won't move on warrants below 0.35% AND won't restructure the prepayment penalty. The extra capital isn't worth it if the all-in cost exceeds $0.70 per dollar borrowed.

## Recommendation

**Proceed with Westbridge Capital, contingent on negotiating warrants to 0.30% and improving the prepayment structure.** The additional $2M in term loan capacity plus the $2M revolver provides critical runway insurance during the Series C process. The $8M ARR covenant is safer than Summit's $10M floor. The 12-month draw period matches the realistic Series C timeline.

If Westbridge won't negotiate, Summit is a clean, simple deal with lower total cost. But you're buying less insurance.
