# Enterprise Expansion Strategy

**2026-04-08 | Jordan Rivera**

## Recommendation

**Launch a dedicated enterprise tier targeting multi-entity businesses with 50+ finance team members by Q3 2026.** 40% of our 87 customers already operate multiple entities. The enterprise motion is the fastest path from $12.5M to $20M ARR this year.

## Context

Our $20M ARR goal requires $7.5M in net new ARR. Our current ACV is $145K. Winning 52 new logos at that ACV is unrealistic in 9 months. But expanding existing multi-entity customers and landing larger groups can close the gap with fewer, bigger deals.

Three signals confirm the timing:

1/ **40% of customers are multi-entity.** They're already asking for centralized admin, cross-entity analytics, and volume pricing. We're losing expansion revenue because we can't sell them what they need.

2/ **Enterprise deals are in the pipeline.** Sarah Chen's team has 6 opportunities above $300K ACV that are stalled because we lack multi-entity admin features.

3/ **SOC 2 Type II is on track.** Devon Park's initiative removes the compliance blocker that disqualifies us from most enterprise RFPs.

## Analysis

**Revenue model:** Enterprise tier at $200K+ ACV. Target 15 enterprise deals in the first 12 months. Blended with SMB, this gets us to $20M ARR.

**What we need to build:**
- Multi-entity admin console (centralized user management, entity-level permissions)
- Cross-entity analytics dashboard
- Custom SLA and support tiers
- Volume pricing engine

**What we don't need to build:** The core product works. Enterprise customers need admin tooling around it, not a different product.

**Competitive window:** Legacy ERPs (NetSuite, Sage Intacct) are losing mid-market businesses frustrated with generic workflows. Vertical finance SaaS competitors (Ramp, Brex) don't have our AI capabilities. We have 12-18 months before they close the gap.

## Risks

**Engineering bandwidth.** The Agent SDK is Marcus Webb's priority. Enterprise admin features compete for the same team. Mitigation: scope the MVP tightly and dedicate 3 engineers (not the SDK team) to enterprise.

**Sales cycle length.** Enterprise deals take 3-6 months. If we start now, first revenue lands in Q3-Q4. This is tight against the annual goal. Mitigation: lead with expansion of existing customers (shorter cycle) while building pipeline for new enterprise logos.

**Feature creep.** Enterprise customers ask for everything. Mitigation: Priya Sharma owns the feature list. Three features, not thirty. Admin console, analytics, volume pricing. Ship by end of Q2.
