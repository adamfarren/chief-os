# LinkedIn Style Guide Reference

## Quick Reference Card

### Do
- Short declarative sentences
- Numbered format: 1/ 2/ 3/
- Bold key claims
- Credit people by first name
- URLs in comments only
- Lead with authority-establishing hooks
- End with insight or takeaway

### Don't
- Bullet points in posts
- Em-dashes
- Headers in posts
- Questions as hooks
- "I've been thinking about..."
- Engagement-bait closings ("agree?", "what do you think?")
- Anthropomorphize AI

### Banned Phrases
- "flexible"
- "AI-first"
- "modern"
- "it's not X, it's Y" (antithesis pattern)
- "in today's world"
- "at the end of the day"

### Content Archetypes
1. **Product Showcase** — Problem → old approach → what we built → result → strategic meaning
2. **Strategic Insight** — Observation → framework → evidence → implication → proof point
3. **Founder Journey** — Specific moment → what happened → lesson → broader principle
4. **Industry Analysis** — News → consensus view → what they're missing → your take

### Character Limits
- LinkedIn post max: ~3,000 characters
- Optimal length: 1,200-2,000 characters
- Hook (first line visible before "see more"): ~140 characters
